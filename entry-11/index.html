'pm' analog archives

at it's core, pm a collection of experiments in personal philisophy and testaments to the analog arts. in my corner of the web, reader, this is pretty huge; but the web extends well beyond this corner. and the world goes even further. (and appreciation for the analog arts grows more and more obscure with every step.)

 

but it is out there.

 

making it matter is an ongoing extension of pm designed to document the work of all who fight to extend the corner. as much as i love the quaintness of my space, i'd be remiss to not share the pockets of analog joy out there, and by proxy, the incredible folks/organizations who've dedicated their lives to honoring and uplifting physical ephemera.

grolier club

 

founded in 1884, they were the first on the pm scene.

 

the club is both a place and an idea. to quote it's manifesto,

penned by founder robert hoe (NYC-printing-press-manufacturer-and-bibliophile):

 

"the club exists to foster the study, collecting, and appreciation of books and works on paper, their art, history, production, and commerce. it shall pursue this mission through the maintenance of a library devoted to all aspects of the book and graphic arts and especially bibliography; through the occasional publication of books designed to illustrate, promote and encourage the book and graphic arts; through exhibitions and educational programs for its members and the general public."
